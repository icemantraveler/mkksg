Grounded/Quick Recovery Attacks:
Press □/Δ/X/O or U/D/F/B when grounded to perform a Quick Recovery.
If you don't press any buttons, you'll do a normal recovery.
Holding R2 results in a Delayed Recovery.
Certain special moves performed on the ground can trigger a Recovery Attack, which is invincible for the first 8 frames.
These attacks will hit opponents who try to attack you during that time.

Jump Attacks:
Use Jump Attacks to hit airborne opponents.
They can knock the opponent down, start a chain combo, or initiate a juggle combo.

Super Meter:
Your Super Meter (3 sections, bottom of the screen) fills by performing special moves, getting hit, or having your attacks blocked.
It powers Enhanced Moves (1 bar), Breakers (2 bars), and X-Ray Attacks (3 bars).
Managing your meter depends on reading your opponent, your health, and attack setups.
Some players use it quickly, while others save for Breakers or X-Rays.

Tag Attacks:
Tag Attacks let your partner join the match and perform a special attack, throw a projectile, or set up a combo.
Your partner cannot be damaged during their attack.

Enhanced Moves:
Press R2 while performing a special move to Enhance it, consuming 1 bar of your Super Meter.
Enhanced moves are more powerful and sometimes alter the move's properties (e.g., Jade's shadow flash becomes invincible).

X-Ray Attacks:
Press (X+O+R2) or (L2+R2) when your Super Meter has 3 bars.
X-Ray attacks can be part of combos and juggle the opponent afterward.
They're powerful, with armor for the first 2 frames, and will ignore up to 3 attacks from your opponent.
If you attempt an X-Ray with no health left, the first attack from your opponent will end the round.

High Attacks:
These hit standing opponents but miss ducking ones.
They're often used in chain combos or mid-screen juggles.
Don't assume they always hit high - some may look like they do but actually don't.

Medium Attacks:
Medium attacks hit standing or ducking opponents.
They're critical for breaking your opponent's defense, especially in chain combos.
Some medium attacks may look like low attacks, causing opponents to block low, making them vulnerable to other attacks.

Low Attacks:
Low attacks require crouching to block.
They can knock down the opponent or slightly stun them.
Low attacks are effective after high attacks or as combo starters.

Overhead Attacks:
These hit ducking opponents who are blocking low.
Some overheads can also pop the opponent into the air.
Use overheads to force your opponent to block high, then follow up with low attacks or grabs.

Pop-Up Attacks:
Pop-up attacks launch opponents into the air, allowing for follow-up juggle combos.
You can also pop up by jumping and pressing □/Δ.

Uppercuts:
Uppercuts are anti-air moves to stop opponents from jumping.
Press (D+Δ). Some characters' uppercuts have faster start-ups.

Combo Starter:
Jump in with □/Δ to start chain combos.
This is a great way to pressure your opponent and open up combo opportunities.

Chain Combo:
A chain combo is a sequence of predetermined inputs for multiple hits.
For example, press □, Δ, X in succession.
To help with timing, hold down the last button after pressing the second one.
Combo effectiveness depends on speed, recovery time, and how they mix high, medium, and low attacks.

Special Move Cancels:
After an attack, you can cancel it into a special move for more confusion or a combo extension.
For example, after pressing Crouching O, quickly input Jade's shadow kick motion.

Corner:
Being in the corner allows for high damage combos but also makes you vulnerable.
If you're on defense, try to escape the corner as soon as possible.

Breakers:
Use Breakers (when you have 2 bars on your Super Meter) to stop an opponent's combo.
Hold (F+R2) to perform a Breaker. Some characters can perform special moves after a successful Breaker.

Throws:
Press R1 to throw your opponent.
You can also perform a forward throw by pressing (F+R1) or a back throw by pressing (B+R1).
Ducking opponents can avoid throws, but they're vulnerable to medium and low attacks unless blocking.

Dash Canceling & Block Canceling:
To dash cancel, input F, F after a move. To block cancel, press (F+R2).
These techniques help you perform complex combos or move quickly between offense and defense.

Juggles & Air Combat:
Juggling is crucial in Mortal Kombat. Perform a standing □, then follow up with special moves for juggle combos.
Experiment with different moves for extended juggles.

Block Damage & Damage Scaling:
Block damage favors aggressive players, as any attack hitting a blocking opponent deals damage.
Damage from repeated attacks (e.g., chain combos or special moves) scales down, but changing your combo resets it.
Damage remains the same if the opponent is blocking.

Tag Combos & Fast Tags:
In Tag mode, press TG to tag your partner into the match. You can also use Tag Attacks or Tag Assists.
Each character has unique tag combos and assists, which are crucial for creating combos and mix-ups.
Use fast tags to safely swap characters and extend your combos.
Babalities: You must not press R2 in the final round.

Finisher Distances Explained:
Close: Right next to your opponent.
Sweep: A couple of steps away (close enough to land a sweep).
Jump: One jump distance away from your opponent.
Fullscreen: As far apart as possible (opponent at the other side of the screen).
Anywhere: Distance doesn't matter, can be any range (Sweep or further works best).
Varies: For some reason, the Stage Fatalities in this game have their distance listed as "Varies". However, regardless of the label, all Stage Fatalities should be performed at Close range.

Note: When (Hold R2) is shown in italics for Fatality inputs, it means that holding R2 is optional.
The Fatality will still work without holding R2, though it may be easier if you do.
Just remember to release R2 before pressing the final button in the Finisher combo.
