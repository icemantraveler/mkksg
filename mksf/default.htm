Default Controls:

X = Low Punch/Action
□ = High Punch/Fire
Δ = High Kick/Climb
O = Low Kick
L1 = Look/Aim
R1 = Block
L2 = Draw Weapon
R2 = Turbo (While holding R2, Pressing one of the face buttons performs a special move)
Select = Inventory
Start = Pause/Quit Menu

Climb = Δ + the directional buttons.
Fire Guns = L1 to Aim, □ to fire.
Zooming with Sniper Rifle = (Zoom In: L1+Δ / Zoom Out: L1+X)

Special Moves and Combos:

Jax has 4 special moves triggered by holding R2 and pressing any punch or kick button.
You can only perform them when the blue bar is at least partially filled.
The default buttons for the special moves are:

(R2+X) = Light Ground Pound
(R2+□) = Hard Ground Pound
(R2+O) = Light Dash Punch
(R2+Δ) = Hard Dash Punch

Combos are button sequences that unleash chains of punches and kicks.
Performing combos fills the blue bar, which lets you use special moves.
At the start of the game, combos aren't available - you unlock them by earning experience points.
The further you progress, the more experience you gain.

Intro:

One of the earliest stories that established the Mortal Kombat universe was the relationship between Jax and Kano.
Jax, a Special Forces agent, was hot on the trail of career criminal Kano, when he was led into the Mortal Kombat tournament.

Kano represented the criminal underworld and Mortal Kombat was a supernatural event that caused all of these things to collide.
But, we've only hinted at their shaded past.

What events caused Jax to despise Kano so much?
How did Kano himself come to be involved with Shang Tsung?
Why is Jax so bent on stopping Kano's gang?
Is it the Black Dragon?

There's a story to be told here and one that will allow new elements of gameplay, while remaining true to the myths that make up the Mortal Kombat universe.

Bars:

At the start of the game, you'll see two bars in the top-left corner:
White for health and Blue for special moves.
Using Jax's special moves depletes the blue bar, which refills by landing combos.
The enemy's health bar appears in the top-right.

Also in the top-right are your experience points and the level at which new combos unlock.
Newly earned combos appear on-screen and in your inventory.
Your weapon ammo is shown in the bottom-right corner.

Weapons & Ammo:

Detonator: Powerful bomb with three second delay.
Grenade Launcher: Useful crowd control weapon.
Machine Gun: 9mm automatic weapon.
Rocket Launcher: Long distance power house weapon.
Shotgun: Deadly close range weapon.
Sniper Rifle: Single-shot rifle for long distance shots.

You'll find various types of ammo for different weapons.
The 9mm clip is for your machine gun, your standard weapon.
Sniper cartridges are great for taking out unaware enemies from a distance.
Shotgun shells pack a punch but come with a slow reload time.

More powerful weapons like the grenade and rocket launchers are excellent for groups of enemies...
...but their ammo - grenades and rockets - are rare, so save them for bosses.
P.S. The detonator can also be used as a weapon.

Enemies:

The enemies in this game have A.I that is similar to the I.Q of a village idiot, making them generally easy to kill...
...except in areas where they overwhelm you. Avoiding them is usually best.
The real challenge comes from the formidable bosses.

Cheats:

1. After you saved the game at the ending of the last level,
you can access the cheat option in Game Settings.
After you enable it, you can start a new game with all the combos
and all the weapons with 999 ammo!
But you won't get any extra Medipaks here.

2. After turning the cheat menu on, start a new game.
While playing, press X (3) and □ (3).
Both your bars will now get filled.
