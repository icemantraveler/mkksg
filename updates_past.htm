03/04/17 - MKKSG Update: So if anyone has bookmarked mortalkombat.co.nf as the new web address for MKKSG "Mortal Kombat - Komplete Strategy Guide", I want to apologize and let you know that after much consideration and an unpleasant experience with the new website host, I have decided to keep MKKSG on Angelfire, where it's been since 2003 or earlier when it was called the "Mortal Kombat Deadly Alliance Unofficial Strategy Guide". It was born on Angelfire, it will perhaps die on Angelfire... But not anytime soon...
I decided to move the site out of necessity, I was running out of disk space. I'm still running out of disk space, LOL. As of this moment the site is sitting on 19.6MB used out of 20MB total. That was the service offered by Angelfire when I signed up in 2003 or so. And I have managed to use that space for the last 14 years without much hassle.
What made me want to start the website in the first place was at the time I couldn't find any accurate movelists for MK Deadly Alliance on the internet at all, so I said, OK, I'll make one for myself. But after getting it set up I realized that it might come in handy for other fans of the series.
But what started off as a guide for Mortal Kombat Deadly Alliance eventually led into MK Deception, and then I started hosting my Mortal Kombat Sims skins for the original "The Sims" game. Man what a long time ago that was... The Sims Skins gave me a lot of attention back in the day, so I was under the impression that the site needed a gimmick to stay effective, so I tried a multitude of things before realizing that what people wanted and needed most of all was accurate movelists.
So 14 years later, I think for the most part we have accurate movelists covered for the majority of the Mortal Kombat series.
I know there's at least one person in the back saying, "But you don't have MK3 EXTRA 60 for NES, Turbo Mortal Kombat III for NES, or MK3 Mortal Kombat Special 56 Pepoles"... Yeah ok, if I wanted to be super meticulous, I could cover them... But no... Just no.
So I started branching out and doing movelists for some of my other favorite fighting games, such as the ClayFighter series, Injustice, Killer Instinct, Primal Rage, and a few lesser known titles that I thought deserved a look at...
And fans enjoyed that, however I felt like I was getting into a different territory with those guides that was outside the "realm" of Mortal Kombat. Pun intended. So I questioned myself. "Where are we going?"...
8 Months ago I got my answer.
I wish I could show it off now, however there's a few things I want to get worked out before showing it off, so stay tuned.

Speaking of Injustice, have you played the demo yet? Got a chance to play it on the XBOX 360 with my buddy Blade, and I have to tell you, Gameplay Vids do not do the game Justice... "Gameplay Vids do Injustice an Injustice"... anyhoo... Yeah, the game looks great, the environments and characters really pop out, and the animations and cinematics are top notch...

Gameplay wise, the game feels like a good balance of MK and SF'ish mechanics, leaning ever so slightly more in the MK direction (Which is a good thing, trust me). The face buttons house the 3 Attack Buttons, (High, Mid, Low), and your character trait button, whose function changes depending on the character. At first I was suspecting that placement of the Character Trait button was a bad idea, and when first picking the game up, I accidentally hit it trying to attack at one point, but this was quickly rectified and did not happen again. My buddy Blade was quick to do the same thing playing as Luthor. Directional throws worked good but lacked variety, and the Environmental Interaction button was hard to trigger at times. Stage transitions were initiated by holding Back and the A button, which started a charge type attack which felt like it took forever. The overall speed of the game is faster than MK (IMO) and maybe even faster than SF4 (Been awhile since I played that tho...) So anyone complaining about the game being too slow - totally unfounded. At times it even seemed like the game was too fast, jump attacks felt a little clunky at times, and Wonder Woman's floating felt odd. The Super Moves were easy to activate, however the Special Meter IMO was a bit hard to read. The Clash/Wager System didn't appear very often, and when it did, it totally confused me, my attention was directed at the characters, and not the meter, so I had no idea what button I was hitting to do what, lol. When I have more time I will try to go into more detail with that system, figuring it out myself in the process... Pausing the game brought up the special moves for the character... but not the entire special move list... You actually have to dig deeper into the menus to find the entire movelist... And you could also activate special moves to appear on your screen during gameplay... but only 6... and Luthor for instance had more than 6 moves... Weird. (Good thing you have my guide to use right?... Right.) Um... Yeah, so that was what I picked up from the demo, and although most of what I've listed may seem negative, I have to say that the game is VERY FUN to play. Blade (playing the game first) beat the Demo with Batman, and I beat the Demo with Wonder Woman, and then we took turns with Luthor. After that, I went through the movelists of each character, and then made a playthrough with Batman myself. If anything, the Demo makes me want to play the full game even more.
